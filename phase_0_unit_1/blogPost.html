Favorite Websites Analysis

Brennon Williams, Dev Bootcamp Week One

Hacker News

I love Hacker News because it leverages it's abnormally intelligent community to find and curate high quality news and information. Hacker News is basically Reddit without the trolls and cat memes. It's main page is incredibly minimalistic. It is designed to emphasize one thing: the content. There are no advertisements, no unnecessary clutter, nothing to detract from the singular purpose of the website. Unlike Reddit, I feel like I actually get something out of Hacker News. Every day I select the two or three most interesting articles and read them. As I mentioned earlier its a serious website, but its consistent. The tone has not changed over the 3ish years I've followed it.

Apple

Apple has a huge number of products, so there's a heavy burden on its website. It needs to be easy to access information on any product quicky or make a purchase. They've solved this problem effectively by organizing the top bar by product ("iPhone, iPad, etc") versus the traditional "Home, About Us, etc." design. It is colorful, but not excessively so. They link their message to their site directly by making their latest advertisement part of the background. The headline "What will your verse be?" sums up their marketing effort. It's very well-done, if a little disturbing (the part I find troubling is the message of the advertisement, which is "buy an iPad and your life will be meaningul, you will 'contribute a verse.'"). Navigation is incredibly easy - there's something on the front page that represents everything a customer would need (for instance, for iPad troubleshooting the user knows to just click "iPad," at the top. Troubleshooting is then only one click away). The Apple website's biggest strength is not as a seller itself, but as a compliment to its storefront. There is no "Buy Now" button anywhere: they want to project the same level of casual minimalism as their retail outlets do.

Hulu

Hulu has an enormous amout of content, including thousands of shows, movies, video game trailers, and clips. When a user visits hulu.com they need to immediately know what's new that they haven't seen yet. Hulu automatically keeps track of the shows you watch, so visitors are alerted by a new episode of a show they watch by the green "New" banner. The top carousel is also a great way to present content becuase it highlights the latest, biggest updates like new popular shows or episodes being added. If I want to see what's new on TV, I can know in under 30 seconds on Hulu's front page. Another really intelligent thing the Hulu designers do is show relevant content under the video you're watching. This keeps the user entertainied as he or she never has to dig for now content, its presented to them.
